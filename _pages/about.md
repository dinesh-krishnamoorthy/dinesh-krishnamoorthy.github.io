I am currently a PhD Candidate at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology under the supervision of Prof. Sigurd Skogestad. I graduated from Imperial College London in 2012 and started working as a Researcher in Equinor research center in Porsgrunn, Norway in the field of process control and optimization. In total, I have about 7 years of research experience with process modelling, control and optimization, both in academia and industry. In this time, I have published 7 Journal papers (  2 more journal papers are currently under review), 19 peer-reviewed conference papers and 2 patents.

My current research objective is to understand the challenges with the traditional steady-state real-time optimization (RTO) approach and how to address some of these challenges. I am presently working on various approaches to online process optimization under uncertainty, including new approaches to real-time optimization, machine learning based optimization approaches, robust multistage economic model predictive control and extremum seeking control.

As a part of my ongoing research work, I have proposed different alternative approaches to traditional steady-state RTO, that effectively uses transient measurements for optimization. I have also been developing different approaches to real time optimization that does not require the need to solve numerical optimization problems, thus alleviating many of the challenges which impede practical application of traditional RTO approaches. My research work has also led to an overall understanding of the different approaches to online process optimization in terms of the degree of complexity and flexibility of each approach, the different timescales they work in and the different kinds of uncertainty that they can handle. My ultimate research goal is to combine the different approaches in a hierarchical fashion that exploits the timescale of the different methods in order to handle a wide class of uncertainties.

In addition to my academic research, I also have more than 4 years of industrial research experience in Equinor Research center, where I have been mainly working on modelling, control and optimization of offshore oil and gas production systems.
